## E

### Espresso Machine
Carlton was the launchpad for Australia's famous coffee culture. The country's very first commercial espresso machines were installed along Lygon Street in the 1950s by Italian immigrants, changing how Melburnians drank coffee forever.

### Exhibition Building (Royal)
Completed in 1880 for the Melbourne International Exhibition, this grand monument features a soaring dome inspired by the Florence Cathedral. It holds a UNESCO World Heritage listing and hosted the opening of the first Parliament of Australia in 1901.

[Back to Top](#encyclopedia-index-a-to-z-carlton-melbourne-edition)

## F

### Faraday Street National School
Opened in 1858, this was one of Carlton's earliest institutions and operated continuously as a state school until 1972. The historic brick building remains a prominent architectural feature of the streetscape.

### Fiesta (Lygon Street)
Launched in 1978, the Lygon Street Fiesta was Australia's first major multicultural street festival. It celebrates Carlton's rich Italian heritage with food, music, and the famous waiters' race.

[Back to Top](#encyclopedia-index-a-to-z-carlton-melbourne-edition)

## G

### Garment Workshops
During the late 19th and early 20th centuries, Carlton was a hub for small-scale textile manufacturing. Many historic back-lane warehouses originally operated as "sweatshops" before the area gentrified into residential terraces.

### Grattan Street
A major east-west thoroughfare that cuts through the heart of Carlton, acting as the main civic link between the University of Melbourne campus and the Lygon Street dining strip.

[Back to Top](#encyclopedia-index-a-to-z-carlton-melbourne-edition)

## H

### Hochgurtel Fountain
The grand, ornate fountain situated on the southern lawn of the Royal Exhibition Building. Designed by Joseph Hochgurtel for the 1880 International Exhibition, it features intricate mythical figures and local flora motifs.

### Hoddle Grid (Extension)
Surveyed by Robert Hoddle in 1852, Carlton was intentionally planned as an orderly extension of Melbourne's grid system. This wide, geometric street plan intentionally included generous public garden squares to prevent overcrowding.

[Back to Top](#encyclopedia-index-a-to-z-carlton-melbourne-edition)

## I

### IMAX Melbourne
Located within the Melbourne Museum complex in the Carlton Gardens, this cinema features one of the largest movie screens in the world, measuring 32 meters wide by 23 meters high.

### Italianate Architecture
The dominant 19th-century architectural style found throughout Carlton's famous residential streets, recognizable by decorative cast-iron lacework, high parapets, and grand double-storey terrace facades.

[Back to Top](#encyclopedia-index-a-to-z-carlton-melbourne-edition)

## J

### Jewish Community Heritage
Before establishing itself as "Little Italy" post-WWII, Carlton was the vibrant heart of Melbourne's Jewish life from the 1920s to the 1940s. The neighborhood was filled with Yiddish theatres, kosher bakeries, and synagogues.

### Jimmy Watson's Wine Bar
A legendary Lygon Street landmark opened in 1935. The building was famously remodeled in 1962 by iconic architect Robin Boyd and remains a foundational institution for Australia's modern wine culture.

[Back to Top](#encyclopedia-index-a-to-z-carlton-melbourne-edition)

## K

### King & Godfree
One of Carlton’s longest-running businesses, operating as a licensed grocer on the corner of Lygon and Faraday Streets since 1871. It played a massive role in introducing European deli goods, olive oil, and wine to local shoppers.

[Back to Top](#encyclopedia-index-a-to-z-carlton-melbourne-edition)

## L

### La Mama Theatre
A cornerstone of independent Australian theater since 1967. Located in a historic former printing works on Faraday Street, La Mama famously nurtured early career work for legendary local playwrights, actors, and comedians.

### Lygon Street
Nationally recognized as Melbourne's "Little Italy," this bustling strip is packed with legacy pizzerias, trattorias, gelaterias, and bookstores, making it the cultural pulse of the suburb.

[Back to Top](#encyclopedia-index-a-to-z-carlton-melbourne-edition)

## M

### Melbourne Museum
A massive, modern museum located in the Carlton Gardens. It holds major natural and cultural history collections, including a living rainforest gallery, dinosaur fossils, and the preserved body of Australia's legendary racehorse, Phar Lap.

### Monkey Grip
Helen Garner's seminal 1977 Australian cult-classic novel, which vividly depicts the bohemian, share-house counter-culture of Carlton and its surrounding inner-north suburbs during the 1970s.

[Back to Top](#encyclopedia-index-a-to-z-carlton-melbourne-edition)

## N

### Nicholson Street
Marking the eastern boundary of Carlton (separating it from Fitzroy), this major thoroughfare has carried historic tram lines since the late 19th century and features beautifully preserved Victorian-era building fronts.

### Nova (Cinema)
An iconic arthouse cinema complex on Lygon Street. Starting as a small twin-screen theater in the 1990s, Cinema Nova has grown into a massive cultural hub for international film, film festivals, and local independent cinema.

## O

### Old Carlton Brewery
Established in Bouverie Street in 1864, this site was the birthplace of Carlton Ale. While most of the massive industrial complex was cleared in the late 1980s, its historic bluestone offices remain standing as a heritage landmark.

[Back to Top](#encyclopedia-index-a-to-z-carlton-melbourne-edition)

## P

### Princes Park
A vast 39-hectare parkland spreading across Carlton's northern border. It features open sports ovals, mature avenues of elm trees, and the historic Ikonic oval home ground of the local football club.

## Q

### Queensberry Street
A key link road tracking along Carlton's southern edge, bordering the Central Business District. It famously hosted part of the marathon route during the Melbourne 2006 Commonwealth Games.

## R

### Rathdowne Street
A beautifully wide, tree-lined boulevard famous for its grand double-story Victorian terraces. Its northern stretch features a charming village atmosphere complete with neighborhood bistros, delis, and cafes.

### Readings Bookstore
An independent literary institution opened on Lygon Street in 1969. It serves as a central meeting place for Melbourne's writing community and won the International Bookstore of the Year award in 2016.

[Back to Top](#encyclopedia-index-a-to-z-carlton-melbourne-edition)

## S

### Squares (Garden Squares)
Carlton is unique for its collection of 19th-century public garden squares built on a classic European model. These rectilinear green spaces include Lincoln Square, Argyle Square, Macarthur Place, and University Square.

### Summer of the Seventeenth Doll
Ray Lawler’s foundational 1955 Australian play, which forever tied the neighborhood to working-class identity. The story takes place entirely inside the living room of a typical double-story Carlton terrace house.

[Back to Top](#encyclopedia-index-a-to-z-carlton-melbourne-edition)

## T

### Tiamo
A family-run Lygon Street institution that has been serving up traditional, fuss-free Italian comfort food and espresso since the mid-20th century, cementing it as an anchor of the neighborhood's dining identity.

### Trades Hall
Located on the corner of Lygon and Victoria Streets, the Melbourne Trades Hall opened in 1859 and is the world's oldest continuously active trade union building. It was the historical nerve center for the campaign that won the 8-hour working day.

[Back to Top](#encyclopedia-index-a-to-z-carlton-melbourne-edition)

## U

### University of Melbourne
Established in 1853 on Carlton's western fringe, this is Australia's second-oldest university. Its historic sandstone Gothic structures and sprawling campus have heavily shaped Carlton's student and academic population for generations.

[Back to Top](#encyclopedia-index-a-to-z-carlton-melbourne-edition)

## V

### Victorian Gold Rush
The explosive 1851 gold rush triggered a massive population boom in Melbourne. Carlton was rapidly subdivided during this exact window to house influxes of worldwide immigrants looking to settle within walking distance of the city center.

[Back to Top](#encyclopedia-index-a-to-z-carlton-melbourne-edition)

## W

### Wurundjeri Woi-wurrung
The Traditional Owners and original inhabitants of the land that Carlton is built upon. The Wurundjeri people of the Kulin Nation have maintained a deep cultural, spiritual, and physical connection to this landscape for tens of thousands of years.

## X

### X-Ray Department (Royal Women's Hospital)
The Royal Women's Hospital, which operated out of its historic Carlton campus on Grattan Street from 1856 until moving in 2008, pioneered early maternal and specialized gynecological imaging and radiology systems in Australia.

## Y

### Yarra Tram Routes
Carlton’s historic grid layout is tightly woven around Melbourne's iconic tram network. Legacy lines like the Route 1 and Route 6 trams have rumbled down Swanston Street and through Carlton North along Lygon Street for over a century.

## Z

### Zone (Capital City Zone)
The town-planning land-use classification applied to Carlton's southern fringes. It marks the transition where the low-rise historic character of Carlton's 19th-century streetscapes meets the high-density commercial landscape of Melbourne's CBD.
